About

The 1st APPI Summer School on Process Intelligence

The Asia-Pacific Process Intelligence (APPI) Summer School is the premier forum for exploring the intersection of process mining, data science, and AI in the region. Over two intensive days, a diverse community of participants and speakers from across the globe come together to learn and develop new approaches in the field. The curriculum covers process discovery and conformance checking in depth, alongside essential topics in data preprocessing, performance analysis, predictive analytics, and the ethical dimensions of responsible process mining. Beyond the lectures, participants engage in interactive hands-on sessions with both open-source and commercial process mining tools to apply theory to real-world scenarios.

Meet the Lecturers

From the founder of process mining to the industry experts behind today's leading tools and research leaders across the Asia-Pacific — the faculty shaping the present and future of process intelligence.

WA RWTH Aachen
Wil van der Aalst
RWTH Aachen University · Germany

Intro to Process Mining · Process Discovery · Conformance Checking

Widely known as the "Godfather of Process Mining," Prof. van der Aalst leads the Process and Data Science (PADS) group at RWTH Aachen University and serves as Chief Scientist at Celonis. One of the most-cited computer scientists in the world and an IFIP, IEEE, and ACM Fellow, he is the author of the seminal book "Process Mining: Data Science in Action."

Curriculum

Lecture Overview

From foundational theory to hands-on practice, AI, and real-world case studies — here is how the two days unfold.

01
Day 1RWTHWil van der Aalst

Unlocking Process Intelligence: A 360° Introduction to Process Mining

A panoramic view of what process mining is and why it matters — how insight about processes is drawn from event data. Introduces the arc from process discovery to conformance checking and performance analysis, and how it is expanding into today's process intelligence.

02
Day 1RWTHWil van der Aalst

From Event Logs to Process Logic: The Basics of Process Discovery

Covers the foundations of process discovery — automatically deriving process models from event logs. Through representative discovery algorithms and process representations such as Petri nets, it walks step by step through how raw logs become intelligible process logic.

03
Day 1RWTHWil van der Aalst

Conformance Checking: Token-Based Replay and Alignments

Conformance checking measures how well what actually happened (the event log) matches what should happen (the process model). Using token-based replay and alignment techniques, participants learn to diagnose process deviations and violations quantitatively.

04
Day 1QUTMoe Wynn

Process Data (Quality Management)

Reliable process mining starts with good data. This session diagnoses the quality issues common in event data — missing, inaccurate, and incomplete records — and introduces practical approaches to cleaning and correcting data before analysis.

05
Day 1CelonisHands-OnPoornima Dhall

Process Mining Hands-On A (Celonis)

A hands-on session working directly with the Celonis platform on real event data. From data connection to process visualization and the detection of bottlenecks and inefficiencies, participants learn to implement process intelligence with a commercial tool.

06
Day 1SAP SignavioHands-OnJulian Lebherz

Process Mining Hands-On B (SAP Signavio)

A hands-on session using SAP Signavio to analyze processes and surface improvement opportunities. Participants apply the theory from earlier sessions on top of a commercial process intelligence tool, building a feel for enterprise use.

07
Day 2UNISTMarco Comuzzi

Process Mining and AI

Explores where process mining meets artificial intelligence. From predictive process monitoring to machine-learning-based analysis, it examines how AI extends process intelligence — and how the two fields reinforce one another.

08
Day 2HUFSBernardo Nugroho Yahya

Federated Process Mining

Introduces federated process mining — jointly analyzing process data distributed across multiple organizations without pooling it in one place. Covers approaches that enable collaborative process analysis while preserving privacy.

09
Day 2TelkomAngelina Prima Kurniati

Process Mining Case Studies

Through a range of real-world applications, this session shows how theory translates into results. It shares experiences and lessons from domains — such as healthcare — where data quality is especially challenging.

10
Day 2Speaker TBD

Process Mining in Healthcare

Covers how process mining is applied to the complex care and treatment pathways of clinical settings. Introduces cases that improve the quality and efficiency of healthcare services through patient-flow analysis and care-pathway improvement.
